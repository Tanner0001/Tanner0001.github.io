DPW-SOP

Public Works Operations

1.0 Department Mission

The Department of Public Works (DPW) is the primary agency responsible for the health, safety, and functionality of San Andreas' infrastructure. From the towering electrical grids of Los Santos to the rural highways of Blaine County, DPW personnel ensure that the state remains operational. This role is a high-visibility public service position that requires technical precision and an unwavering commitment to safety. You are the first line of defense against infrastructure failure and environmental hazards.

2.0 Roadway Safety (D.O.T.)

The Department of Transportation (DOT) division focuses on the maintained portions of public highways. Your primary duty is the installation, repair, and protection of traffic control devices and road surfaces. Working near active traffic is the most dangerous aspect of this job; therefore, strict adherence to work-zone protocols is mandatory.

Work Zone Setup

When performing repairs, you must establish a 'Safety Taper' using traffic cones. Tapers should begin 100 meters prior to the work site, using a 45-degree angle to gradually shift traffic into adjacent lanes. "Construction Ahead" signage must be placed visible to oncoming drivers to comply with RCSA Title III standards.

Traffic Coordination

For major repairs or bridge inspections, coordinate with the San Andreas Highway Patrol (SAHP) to provide a rolling roadblock or full road closure. Never attempt to stop high-speed traffic with hand signals alone; always utilize vehicle-mounted arrow boards and amber lighting.

3.0 Utility & Grid Response

The Utilities division manages the state’s Power, Water, and Gas systems. Personnel in this role are 'Emergency Responders' and must be prepared to react to infrastructure disasters at a moment's notice.

San Power: Downed Line Protocol

Downed electrical lines represent a Tier-1 life threat. Upon arrival, you must establish a 50-meter perimeter. Do not allow any personnel (including LEO/FD) to touch the affected vehicle or structure until the grid has been confirmed isolated at the nearest substation. If fire is present, coordinate with the Fire Department for specialized non-conductive suppression.

Water & Gas: Leak Management

Gas leaks require immediate evacuation of the surrounding area. Utilize gas-sniffing equipment to determine the saturation levels before attempting a valve-shutoff. Water main breaks require careful excavation to prevent further damage to nearby fiber-optic or electrical lines.

4.0 Sanitation & Environmental Health

Sanitation workers are the primary monitors for environmental health. While performing waste collection routes, you are also acting as a reporter for the state. Under RCSA 6.03 (Illegal Dumping), you are required to document and report any unauthorized disposal of hazardous materials or bulk waste in public spaces.

Illegal Dumping Enforcement:

If you observe a citizen dumping waste into a waterway or on a public highway, do not confront them directly. Record the vehicle plate and notify Law Enforcement immediately. Your documentation is the key to successfully charging violators under Title VI of the RCSA.

5.0 Legal & Safety Compliance

All DPW personnel must adhere to the RCSA at all times. Failure to follow safety standards results in personal liability and potential criminal negligence charges.

  • RCSA 3.16 Compliance: High-visibility vests and protective headgear must be worn at all times while on a public roadway.

  • Lighting Standards: Amber lighting is a tool for safety, not a permit to violate traffic laws. Lights should only be active when stationary or when operating as a slow-moving hazard.

SEC-SOP

Private Security Protocols

1.0 Professional Conduct & Diplomacy

Dynamic Security personnel represent the server's standard for professional civilian roleplay. You must remain calm, patient, and highly observant at all times. Prioritize **Diplomacy over Violence**; weapons are a final resort, not a regular tool of the trade.

Situational Awareness

Maintain a 360-degree awareness. Watch your corners, your front, and your back. Your primary duty is the protection of yourself, your partner, and the property you are assigned to guard.

LEO Cooperation

Comply with and assist Law Enforcement Officers at all times. Security and LEO share the same goal of public safety. Treat peace officers with the utmost respect.

2.0 Command & Supervisory Protocols

Security is one of the premier civilian career paths on the server. Proper command structure ensures that all agencies (Static Guard, CIT, and Private Contractors) operate within server rules and SOPs.

Supervisor Request Policy

If a citizen or LEO requests a security supervisor, the request **must** be facilitated immediately. Even if no supervisor is physically present on-site, personnel must attempt to contact one via the radio frequency.

3.0 Cash-In-Transit (CIT)

CIT units are responsible for the armored transport of high-value assets and currency. This role requires heightened awareness and strict adherence to route protocols to prevent robberies.

3.1 Static & Patrol

Standard guards focused on securing a fixed location or performing designated patrol loops. Focus is on trespassing enforcement and 'Observe & Report' procedures.

4.0 Vehicle & Lighting Standards

Personnel must utilize an authorized agency patrol vehicle while on duty. **Personal vehicles are strictly prohibited for security operations.**

Lighting & Siren Auth

  • Amber Lighting: Standard for all patrol and utility response.
  • Red Lighting: Authorized for high-security property defense and government contract operations.
  • Siren Usage: Authorized ONLY within private property boundaries or during active government escort contracts to preserve life/property.

Prohibited Actions

Never use red lights or sirens on public highways to bypass traffic signals or perform traffic stops. These tools are restricted to private property or approved government escorts. Violations constitute Impersonation of a Government Employee (RCSA 1.06).

Security Use of Force Continuum

Level 1: Verbal Presence and commands to leave.
Level 2: Soft Control Escorting individuals off-property.
Level 3: Hard Control Physical restraint / Handcuffing.
Level 4: Less Lethal Taser/OC (Armed certification required).
Level 5: Lethal Grave threat defense only.
TOW-SOP

Towing & Recovery Operations

1.0 Role Overview

As a Towing & Recovery Specialist, you are the backbone of the state's logistical infrastructure. Your primary objective is to maintain the flow of traffic by clearing disabled vehicles, responding to accidents, and facilitating law enforcement impounds. This role requires a high level of technical skill, safety awareness, and professional diplomacy. You are often the first person a citizen sees after a stressful accident, making you a key representative of the civilian service community. Maintaining a calm and professional demeanor is essential for successful roleplay in this field.

2.0 Operational Standards

Before beginning your shift, you must perform a full vehicle inspection. Ensure your winch, chains, and amber lighting systems are fully operational. While on duty, you must wear high-visibility safety apparel at all times to comply with RCSA 3.16. When arriving at a scene, especially those involving Law Enforcement or Fire/EMS, you must stage your vehicle in a way that protects the scene while allowing for a quick exit. Always check in with the Incident Commander (IC) before touching any vehicle within a designated perimeter. Failure to coordinate can lead to evidence contamination or safety hazards.

3.0 Legal & Safety Guidelines

Safety is the non-negotiable priority of Towing operations. Under RCSA 3.14 and 3.15, you must document all pre-existing damage to a vehicle before it is hooked or loaded. This prevents fraudulent liability claims against your agency. Amber lighting must be activated whenever you are stationary on a public roadway or when transporting a load that significantly limits your speed. Remember: You are a civilian contractor. You have no authority to pursue vehicles or perform traffic stops. Unauthorized use of equipment to interfere with traffic will result in immediate termination and potential criminal charges for Impersonation (RCSA 1.06).

Professional Code

Always provide a receipt for services, treat the public with respect, and coordinate closely with the San Andreas Highway Patrol. Efficiency and safety are the hallmarks of a master tow operator.

ME-SOP

Medical Examiner Manual

1.0 Office of the Medical Examiner

The Office of the Medical Examiner (OME) is a specialized civilian forensic agency. Your primary mandate is to investigate all sudden, violent, or suspicious deaths within the state. Unlike standard EMS, your focus is not on life-saving measures, but on the clinical documentation of the deceased and the collection of physical evidence. You are a scientist in the field, and your findings are often the deciding factor in criminal prosecutions and civil litigation.

2.0 Forensic Scene Preservation

A death scene is a primary crime scene until proven otherwise. Your interaction with the scene must be clinical and documented. Every movement within the "Inner Perimeter" must be authorized by the Lead LEO Investigator on-scene.

Photographic Evidence

Before the body is moved, you must photograph the deceased in their original position. Capture the surrounding environment, including shell casings, weapons, or drug paraphernalia. These "In-Situ" photos are vital for reconstruction.

Chain of Custody

All personal effects found on the deceased (Wallets, Phones, Jewelry) must be bagged and tagged separately. You are responsible for the legal chain of custody until the property is transferred to the LEO evidence locker.

3.0 Identification & Transport

Positive identification is the priority once the scene is secured. If the deceased carries no ID, use fingerprints or distinguishing marks (Tattoos/Scars) for the preliminary report.

Removal & Logistics

  • Utilize a professional remain-transport bag for all removals.
  • Ensure the transport vehicle is staged away from public view to maintain dignity.
  • Log the "Time of Arrival" and "Time of Removal" for the Case File.

4.0 Multi-Agency Coordination

The Medical Examiner works at the intersection of Law Enforcement and Public Health. Your role requires professional communication across all departments.

With LEO

Provide preliminary cause of death (Homicide/Suicide/Accidental) to assist investigators.

With Fire/EMS

Coordinate the "Pronouncement of Death" if EMS was performing life-saving measures.

With Public

Do not release the names of the deceased to the media or public. Refer all inquiries to LEO PIO.

5.0 Professional Integrity

The Medical Examiner’s role is one of immense responsibility. You are representing the state during a citizen’s most tragic moment. Any conduct that compromises the dignity of the deceased or the integrity of an investigation—including "Scene Looting" or unauthorized disclosure of details—will result in immediate permanent removal from the civilian registry and criminal prosecution.
