Section 5

Divisions

Engine Company

Engine Companies form the core of the fire department. Their primary role lies in fire suppression and prevention, utilizing specialized equipment like hoses and pumps. They are also trained to handle medical emergencies and assist with other critical situations.

Basic Life Support (BLS)

EMTs operating within BLS ambulances ensure rapid interventions and patient stabilization before transport. EMTs are not permitted to administer medication of any kind.

Advanced Life Support (ALS)

Paramedics operating within ALS ambulances withhold state certification. Their advanced equipment and knowledge allow for rapid life-saving techniques and the administration of medication.

Water Rescue

Rescue Boats prioritize duties related to marine operations, conducting search and rescue missions and participating in specialized water-borne operations.

Urban Search and Rescue (USAR)

A specialized team trained for complex rescue operations in urban environments. Equipped with search dogs and cutting tools to locate and extract victims from dangerous situations.

Section 4

Agency Patrol Zones

Law enforcement agencies are assigned specific patrol zones within which they are responsible for maintaining public safety and order. Adherence to designated patrol zones is expected, with the exception of responding to mutual aid requests.

In instances where multiple law enforcement agencies share jurisdiction over a particular zone, the agency represented by the designated color holds primary authority. Secondary jurisdiction extends to any other agencies operating within the shared zone.

Cross-Jurisdiction Protocol

When pursuing a suspect and entering a zone outside of their agency's jurisdiction, law enforcement officers are obligated to promptly notify the primary jurisdiction holder of their entry and the circumstances surrounding it.

Section 6

Use of Force Continuum

1. Compliant

Lowest Force

Officer Presence: Use body language and position to deter or de-escalate.

2. Resistant (Passive)

Verbal

Verbal Commands: Clear and concise commands to direct the subject's behavior.

3. Resistant (Active)

Soft Control

Soft Empty-Hand: Technique such as physical compliance holds, wrist locks, and joint locks.

4. Assaultive (Harm)

Hard Control

Hard Empty-Hand / Less Lethal: Strikes, pressure points, takedowns, tasers, and batons.

5. Serious Bodily Harm / Death

Lethal Force

Deadly force as a last resort to save life or prevent serious injury.

6.1 Investigation and Accountability

If an officer is involved in an officer-involved shooting (OIS), officers who fired their weapon must relinquish their firearm at the request of a detective or supervisor. The officers must also serve a statement to the requestor, and return to the station to sign out a new firearm.

Section 7

Traffic Stops

Operational Flow

1

Activate emergency warning system (Lights/Sirens).

2

Update dispatch with status and location.

3

Run vehicle plate and registered owner for warrants.

4

Approach politely, state reason for stop, and request documents.

5

Evaluate behavior for signs of impairment or hostility.

7.1 High Risk Traffic Stops

Initiated upon investigation of a serious crime previously, currently, or about to be committed.

  • Request backup immediately (Minimum 2 additional units).
  • Position secondary vehicle to the left; third vehicle behind/between.
  • Primary unit commands suspect: Keys out, walk backwards, knees down.
  • Clear the vehicle only once all visible occupants are secured.

04 Transport to station/prison and unrestrain once in cell.

Law Enforcement Agencies

Detailed information about each department.

The Los Santos Police Department (LSPD) is the primary law enforcement agency for the City of Los Santos. Its mission is to protect the lives and property of the people of Los Santos, to maintain public order, and to enforce the law with impartiality and integrity.

The Los Santos Port Police (LSPP) is a specialized law enforcement agency responsible for policing the Port of Los Santos and the Los Santos International Airport. Its mission is to ensure the security of the port and airport facilities, and to protect the flow of commerce through these vital transportation hubs.

The Los Santos County Sheriff's Office (LSCSO) is the primary law enforcement agency for the unincorporated areas of Los Santos County and the City of Davis and Rockford Hills. Its mission is to provide professional law enforcement services to the community, and to maintain public safety through proactive policing and community engagement.

The Blaine County Sheriff's Department (BCSD) is the primary law enforcement agency for Blaine County. Its mission is to provide safe and secure communities for the residents and visitors of Blaine County, and to uphold the law with professionalism and respect.

The Paleto Bay Police Department (PBPD) is the primary law enforcement agency for the City of Paleto Bay. Its mission is to maintain a safe and peaceful environment for the community, and to provide professional law enforcement services with integrity and dedication.

The San Andreas State Patrol (SASP) is the primary highway patrol agency for the State of San Andreas. Its mission is to ensure the safety of the state's highways, and to provide assistance to local law enforcement agencies when needed. Other state agencies include San Andreas State Parks and the San Andreas Department of Fish and Wildlife.

Federal agencies include the United States Forest Service Law Enforcement, the National Parks Service, the United States Border Patrol, and the United States Department of Homeland Security. Their mission is to enforce federal laws and regulations within their respective jurisdictions, and to protect the nation's natural resources and borders.

Pursuit Management

Primary Unit Role

Maintains focus on the suspect vehicle. Only primary should call out locations, speeds, and occupant behavior.

Secondary Unit Role

Provides immediate backup. Secondary handles secondary radio traffic, coordinates PIT requests, and prepares for bail-outs.

PIT Authorization

The Pursuit Intervention Technique (PIT) is a tactic used to force a fleeing vehicle to turn sideways abruptly, causing the driver to lose control and stop.

Standard PIT Rule:
  • Authorized below 35 MPH for standard units.
  • Authorized in areas with low pedestrian traffic.
  • Requires supervisor authorization or clear felony threat.
Phase 04

Tactical Engagement

Use of Force Continuum

Level 1: Presence Professional Uniform & Vehicle.
Level 2: Verbal Commands, Warnings, Persuasion.
Level 3: Physical Handcuffing, Restraint, Pressure.
Level 4: Less Lethal Taser, OC Spray, Beanbag.
Level 5: Lethal Firearm (Grave Bodily Threat).

Scenario Response Guides

Active Shooter

Immediate Deployment: Do not wait for SWAT if life is at risk. Form a 4-man diamond stack. Primary objective is to neutralize the threat and secure the area for EMS entry.

Hostage/Barricade

Perimeter Setup: Establish a hard inner perimeter. Do not attempt entry. Designate a negotiator. Entry only authorized if the 'Execution Clock' starts.

Crime Scene Protocol

1

Isolate & Secure: Use yellow tape or vehicles to block civilian access. Establish an entry/exit log.

2

Document Evidence: Photograph every shell casing and weapon in place before collection.

3

Witness Statements: Separate witnesses immediately to prevent story matching.
